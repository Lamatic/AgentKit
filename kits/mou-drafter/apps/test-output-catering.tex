\section*{Recitals}
This Memorandum of Understanding is entered into between \textsc{AEON Student Council} (``\textsc{Engager}'') and \textsc{Spice Route Caterers Pvt. Ltd.} (``\textsc{Vendor}'').

\textsc{Engager} wishes to engage \textsc{Vendor} to provide professional catering services for the AEON 2025 event. \textsc{Vendor} represents that it has the necessary expertise and resources to perform these services and agrees to do so under the terms set out in this agreement.

\textsc{Vendor} will provide comprehensive catering services for the three-day event, including menu planning, a tasting session, and full meal preparation and service for all event days as specified in the deliverables schedule.

\section*{Definitions}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.5em,style=nextline]
  \item[\textsc{Services}] The catering and food preparation services described in the Scope of Work.
  \item[\textsc{Deliverables}] The specific service milestones and outputs listed in the payment schedule.
  \item[\textsc{Total Fee}] The total consideration of 750,000 INR payable for the Services.
\end{description}

\section{Payment and Milestones}
The \textsc{Total Fee} for the \textsc{Services} is 750,000 INR. \textsc{Engager} will pay a deposit of 30\% (225,000 INR) upon execution of this agreement. The remaining balance of 525,000 INR will be paid in four equal instalments of 131,250 INR, each due within 15 days of the \textsc{Engager}'s acceptance of the following \textsc{Deliverables}:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em]
\item Menu planning and tasting session (Due 2025-08-15)
\item Day 1 full catering service (Due 2025-09-12)
\item Day 2 full catering service (Due 2025-09-13)
\item Day 3 full catering service and cleanup (Due 2025-09-14)
\end{itemize}
% PATTERN:payment-milestones

\section{Acceptance Criteria}
\textsc{Engager} will review each \textsc{Deliverable} within five (5) business days of receipt or performance. If \textsc{Engager} does not provide written notice of rejection specifying the reasons for non-acceptance within this window, the \textsc{Deliverable} will be deemed accepted. Rejection may only be based on the failure of the \textsc{Deliverable} to meet the agreed acceptance criteria, such as food safety standards or service timing.
% PATTERN:acceptance-window

\section{Indemnification}
\textsc{Vendor} and \textsc{Engager} will each indemnify and hold the other party harmless from third-party claims arising from their respective performance under this agreement. This indemnity is capped at 750,000 INR (1x the \textsc{Total Fee}). This cap does not apply to claims arising from gross negligence, willful misconduct, intellectual property infringement, or breaches of confidentiality.
% PATTERN:indemnity-mutual-cap

\section{Limitation of Liability}
Neither party will be liable for any indirect, consequential, or punitive damages. Total aggregate liability for either party under this agreement is limited to 750,000 INR. This limitation does not apply to the specific carve-outs identified in the indemnity clause.
% PATTERN:liability-cap

\section{Termination}
Either party may terminate this agreement for convenience by providing 30 days' written notice. Additionally, either party may terminate this agreement for cause if the other party breaches a material term and fails to cure such breach within 7 days of receiving written notice of the breach.
% PATTERN:termination-dual

\section{Force Majeure}
Neither party will be liable for delays or failures in performance caused by events beyond their reasonable control, including pandemics, cyberattacks, government actions, or infrastructure outages. This clause excuses performance but does not excuse the obligation to pay for \textsc{Services} already rendered and accepted.
% PATTERN:force-majeure-carveouts

\section{Confidentiality}
The parties will maintain the confidentiality of all non-public information exchanged during this engagement. This obligation survives the termination of this agreement for a period of 2 years.
% PATTERN:confidentiality-survival

\section{Subcontracting}
\textsc{Vendor} will not subcontract any portion of the \textsc{Services} without the prior written consent of \textsc{Engager}. \textsc{Vendor} remains fully responsible for the performance and conduct of any approved subcontractors.

\section{Insurance Requirements}
